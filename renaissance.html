History

The Renaissance period flourished in Florence, Italy in the 14th century. It is a French word that means "rebirth". It is inevitable to talk about the Renaissance without the Medieval period, a dark time where Europe had a dramatic change socially, politically and economically. During the Medieval times,the economic class was basically the clergy and the peasants which were the poorest people. During the medieval era, nobody was allowed to study math or classical humanism for their paintings which led to unrealistic work of the human body, such as paintings of people with eighteen ribs, or Jesus as a child painted as an adult. Right after the Medieval period ended in Europe, the Renaissane started to appear. It came with a new class, the middle class. The renaissance brought new ideas of painting which portrayed naturality. A Renaissance man was Michelangelo, one of his masterpiece renaissance works is David, a marble sculpture that shows the technique of classical humanism. The second of the great Renaissancess artists was Urbinoborn Raphael, one of his marvelous paintings was The Alba Madonna, which shows harmony, freshness, and talent. Raphael organized the composition according to simple geometric shapes : the triangle, the circle, and the trapezoid. The freedom of learning from the Greeks let artists such as Raphael and Michelangelo offer a different perspective on art.

Paintings

The Last Supper/ 1495 to 1498
It is a mural painting by the Italian Renaissance artist Leonardo da Vinci, who was active as a painter, sculptor, engineer, draughtsman, theorist, architect, and scientist. The painting represents the scene of the Last Supper of Jesus with his Twelve Apostles. Its handling of space, mastery of perspective, treatment of motion, and complex display of human emotion have made it one of the Western world's most recognizable paintings and among Leonardo's most celebrated works.

The Creation of Adam/ 1508 to 1512
It is one out of the 343 figures fresco painting by the Italian artist Michelangelo, which forms part of the Sistine Chapel's ceiling inside the Vatican Museums. The ceiling has 343 figures painted over 500 square metres. It was one of the most complex and difficult paintings to make: it took sixteen days to complete. Michelangelo started with the figure of God and the Angels and later frescoed the figure of Adam.

The School of Athens/ Raphael/ 1509 and 1511
This is one of the main frescos located at the Vatican. The School of Athens depicts ancient and contemporary thinkers and philosophers. The central figures are Aristotle and Plato. Raphael painted himself in the fresco wearing a black cap.

The Birth of Venus. It is a fresco painting by the Italian artist Michelangelo, which forms part of the Sistine Chapel's ceiling. 1508 to 1512
