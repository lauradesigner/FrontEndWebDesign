Renaissance Art

History

The Renaissance period occurred between the 14th and 17th centuries, it was one of the most important development in Europe, known primarily for its impact on the art world, and it is associated with the Italian city of Florence where it flourished. The Renaissance initially began as a cultural movement during the late medieval period in Italy. However, it quickly dispersed all over Europe. Due to this, most other European countries experienced their own version of the Renaissance in terms of their styles and ideas. During this time period, artists studied the human body in unprecedented detail and were able to accurately reflect their knowledge in their artwork; this is why Renaissance artists typically chose to draw, paint, and sculpt extraordinarily realistic and three-dimensional figures, as never seen before.

Artists of that era were encouraged by wealthy families in Italy to focus their studies on ancient Greek and Roman cultures, and they began to finance the creation of magnificent palaces that were filled with paintings, sculptures, and literature that upheld these values. The city of Florence was one of the most important regions during the Italian Renaissance, as most celebrated artworks originated there. The remarkable magnificent artists known as the "Trinity of the High Renaissance" created three of history's most well-known and celebrated paintings and sculptures. The Statue of David by Michelangelo, Mona Lisa by Leonardo da Vinci, and The School of Athens by Raphael.

Paintings

The Last Supper by Leonardo da Vinci
1495 to 1498
It is a mural painting by the Italian Renaissance artist Leonardo da Vinci, who was active as a painter, sculptor, engineer, draughtsman, theorist, architect, and scientist. The painting represents the scene of the Last Supper of Jesus with his Twelve Apostles. Its handling of space, mastery of perspective, treatment of motion, and complex display of human emotion have made it one of the Western world's most recognizable paintings and among Leonardo's most celebrated works.

The Creation of Adam by Michelangelo
1508 to 1512
It is one of the 343 figures painted over 500 square meters by the Italian artist Michelangelo on the Sistine Chapel's ceiling inside the Vatican Museums.  It is the most famous section of the Sistine Chapel ceiling because this piece changed the perception of God during that time. God has always been regarded as a distinct creature from mankind, but Michelangelo painted him as a simple old man shown to be touchable, reachable, and close to mankind.

The School of Athens by Raphael
1509 to 1511
It is regarded as one of Raphael's best known works because of the perfection of perspective projection. It depicts a congregation of philosophers, mathematicians, and scientists from Ancient Greece, including Plato, Aristotle, Pythagoras, Archimedes, and Heraclitus. The central figures are Aristotle and Plato. Raphael painted himself in the fresco wearing a black cap.

The Birth of Venus by Botticelli
1485 to 1486
Alessandro di Mariano di Vanni Filipepi, known as Botticelli, was an Italian painter of the Renaissance period. This painting sparked debate due to his mythological features and nakedness at a time when only biblical paintings were depicted.
